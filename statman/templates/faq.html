FAQ
Q: My team currently pays for spray charts. Is Statman Sprays really free?

Statman Sprays is 100% free to use and presents data that is as accurate as anything else out there. We don't want money to get in the way of teams achieving their full potential.


Q: Where does the data come from?

Statman Sprays pulls publically available play-by-play data from the NCAA stats repository before it is cleaned and parsed to generate spray charts.

Our proprietary algorithm is specifically tailored to handle NCAA play-by-play text strings and is able to correctly capture the batter, location, and outcome for >99% of all BIP. If you think we got something wrong, let us know.


Q: Can I save a copy of a spray chart?

Absolutely, click on the print icon next to a player's name to print that chart or to save it as a PDF. Click on the prompt next to the team name to do the same for the whole team.


Q: Do you adjust for balls put in play against a defensive shift?

No, we assume that all balls are put in play against a traditional defensive allignment.


Q: Some charts seem to change every time I look at them. Why is that?

Balls up the middle are assigned to either the SS or 2B bucket according to that batterâ€™s tendencies. For example, a batter with 30 BIP to SS and 10 to 2B will have balls hit up the middle placed in the SS bucket 75% of the time and in the 2B bucket 25% of the time.

This calculation is performed when the spray chart is drawn and can lead to slight changes.


Q: Can I split the charts out by pitcher side?

Unfortunately, neither batter side nor pitcher side is reported on the NCAA website or consistently on team sites.
