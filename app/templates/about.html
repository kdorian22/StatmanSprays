About Us
Advance scouting can be a challenge for college baseball coaches. Statman Sprays looks to make it a litle bit easier.

Using publically available play-by-play data, Statman Sprays offers free, accurate spray charts for any NCAA baseball player dating back to 2018.

Use our spray charts to understand your opponents and efficiently position your defenders.
How does it work?
Statman Sprays pulls rosters and play-by-play data for any team in the country straight from the NCAA stats repository. That data is cleaned, parsed, and stored in our public database and is available to any Statman Sprays user.

Statman Sprays was developed by a former D3 student manager and current professional software developer who has spent years refining this process to bring you the most accurate and complete data possible.

We give you total control:
  • Visit the UPDATE DB tab to extract data from NCAA stats repository and load it into our database.
  • If someone else has already loaded the data you are looking for, hover over the desired table cell to make sure it's up to date. Otherwise, you're all set.
  • From there, hop on over to the SPRAYS tab to visualize any batter’s batted ball tendencies
    • FAQ
    Q: Can I look at a batter's entire career rather than just 1 year at a time?

    Because there is no unique way to identify players across years, spray charts are currently only available for 1 season at a time.


    Q: Can I split the data out by pitcher side?

    Unfortunately, neither batter side not pitcher side is listed on the NCAA website or consistently on team sites.


    Q: How do you deal with balls in the gap?

    Balls in the gap, ball in the 5-6 or 3-4 holes, and balls up the middle are randomly assigned to one of the two possible field sections according to that batter’s tendencies. For example, a batter with 30 BIP to CF and 10 to LF will have balls hit to LCF placed in CF 75% of the time and in LF 25% of the time.

    This calculation is performed when the spray chart is drawn and can lead to slight changes in individual charts each time it is drawn.

    Q: What happens to balls fielded by the pitcher or catcher?

    Balls fielded by the pitcher or catcher are included in the Inf BIP total, but now in any section of the spray chart.

    Q: Do you adjust for balls put in play against a defensive shift?

    No, we assume that all balls are put in play against a traditional defensive allignment.

    Q: Why don't the hit and out totals match up with the stats I see on other sites?

    Hit and out totals on each spray chart are aggregated from play-by-play strings which occassionally lack BIP locations or outomes.

    We ignore those faulty data points resulting in numbers that don't always line up perfectly with other aggregations.
